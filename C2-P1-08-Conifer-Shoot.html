Conifer Shoot as the Basic Canopy Element

In coniferous canopies the shoot — stem with needles arranged around it — serves as the fundamental scattering element replacing the flat broadleaf
(a) Pine Branch
A pine branch bearing multiple shoots. Each shoot has a woody stem with needles arranged radially around it.
(b) Single Shoot
A single spruce shoot — the basic scattering element. Needle area density: an = ½ × total needle surface area.
(c) Sun & Shade Shoots
Cross-sections showing needle arrangement. Sun shoots have dense, radially distributed needles; shade shoots are sparser.
(d) Shoots as Canopy Elements
Individual shoots scattered in canopy space. Green dashed outlines show the shoot envelope — the effective “leaf” in radiative transfer.
(e) Shoot Orientation Angles
Shoot orientation: θL = angle between Z (zenith) and shoot stem; φL = azimuth of shoot stem projection on ground plane; γ = rotation angle of shoot about its own axis.
