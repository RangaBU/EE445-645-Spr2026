⭐ Stellar Scintillation: Why Stars Twinkle

Atmospheric refraction due to turbulent refractive index variations

Physical Phenomenon:

Stars appear to twinkle because Earth's atmosphere has turbulent pockets of air with different temperatures and densities. These create random variations in the refractive index (n ≈ 1.0003 ± Δn), which bend starlight as it passes through.

Snell's Law: When light crosses boundaries between regions of different refractive indices, it bends according to: n₁sin(θ₁) = n₂sin(θ₂)

Ray deflection: Δθ ≈ (Δn/n) × L/h

where Δn is refractive index variation, L is path length, h is scale height

As atmospheric conditions change, different rays reach the observer, causing the star's apparent brightness to fluctuate - creating the twinkling effect we observe.
